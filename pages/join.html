Interactive Self-Grader

ACMS groups players of similar standards at Playing Days to ensure satisfying ensembles. Take our diagnostic grader below to establish your recommended level.

About Self-Grading

You will be shown a list of pieces for your chosen instrument. The list is not exhaustive; in each case these have been selected from a large repertoire. These are not in any sense "test" pieces and there is no requirement to be able to play these to join the Society. Finally, this is an assessment of basic reading ability, not overall musicianship.

There will be excerpts from each piece so that you can view the piece and decide whether you would be able to play it. You should indicate whether you could play it immediately, whether you would need a few days notice so that you could practise the piece, or whether you feel that it is too advanced for you to play. Once you have made a choice for each piece, click the "Assess" button to view your grade.

You should respond for ALL pieces in each grade category. If you do not select an option then "Not within a week" will be assumed!

Before You Start

Remember that self-grading is not a hard and fast science. Everyone's interpretation of the questionnaire will be personal to some extent. You may like to consider the following explanations, which might help to clarify things for you.

What does "At Sight" mean?

It means that if someone put that piece or one of a similar standard in front of you, you would be able to play it as part of a group. You would not be expected to get all the notes right, but you would expect that the group would be able to play through without stopping.

What does "3-5 days notice" mean?

It means that if someone asked you to play the piece or one of a similar standard, you would need a few days' notice so that you could have a look at it, play it through a few times, go over some harder passages and work out how to play them. It does not expect that you would be note perfect, just that the group would be able to play through the piece without stopping. If some notes are dropped but the group stays together that is fine.

What does "Not within a week" mean?

Simply that you would need to do some serious work on the piece to be able to have a chance at playing it with a group.

What is the Descriptive Self Grader?

If you prefer a more descriptive guide over assessing individual music pieces, the Descriptive Self Grader allows you to select a level based on written descriptions of playing ability. You can access it via the button below.

I don't think I got my grading right, can I do it again?

Yes, of course. After you get your result, you can click the "Retake Test" button to start over.
