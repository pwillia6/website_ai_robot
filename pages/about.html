Introduction to the Society

Formed in 1984 by Irwin Imhof, Nils Korner, and Judy Mitchell, the Society arose out of a fundamental human desire: there must be a huge number of amateur musicians who want to play chamber music, but have no easy way of contacting other players.

Today, the society has grown to about 380 active musicians, spawning sister societies in Melbourne, Adelaide, and Brisbane.

What Standard of Play is Expected?

While we have no minimum standard for general listings, to enjoy our organized Playing Days or Music Camps we highly suggest:

  • General Instrumentalists: Self-Grading of Level 4 or better.
  • Pianists: Self-Grading of Level 3 or better.
  • Sight-reading: Competent, calm sight-reading is expected. If you struggles to read on the fly, we ask that you secure parts to prepare works in advance.

Our Founders & Patron

The visionaries who built our chamber music family.

Nils Korner

Founder & Mastermind of Wollongong Music Camp

Born in Czechoslovakia, Nils settled in Sydney in 1939. He qualified as a diagnostic radiologist, practicing in Cremorne and Brookvale. Music was the great joy of his life. For over 20 years, he played violin in a group meeting fortnightly. He envisioned the Wollongong Weekends of Chamber Music, searching out appropriate locations to allow amateur musicians access to professional environments. He passed away in 2002.

Rachel Valler OAM

ACMS Patron & Celebrated Pianist

One of Australia's most distinguished pianists. Rachel has performed as a soloist with most major Australian symphony orchestras and toured international circuits under Musica Viva. An examiner for the AMEB for several decades, she was awarded the Order of Australia Medal (OAM) for her massive services to music education and community outreach.

Historical Timeline

Tracing ACMS activities from 1984 to 2026.

1984

NSW Amateur Chamber Music Society formed by Irwin Imhof, Nils Korner and Judy Mitchell contacting personal acquaintances and placing advertisements in newspapers and Musica Viva.
First Membership list (63), first meeting and Playing Day at Lane Cove Public School, November 1984. Irwin Imhof, Convener.
Membership grew to 86.

1985

Nils Korner, President.

Membership Fee $10.
Circulation of a membership list twice a year.
Playing Days $17.
Self-grading questionnaire introduced.
John Pinn suggested establishing a library.
Nils Korner commissioned the Society logo.

1986

John Pinn, President and administration.
First Wollongong Music Camp, 44 participants. Played at Gleniffer Brae, lived at Kooloobong. Impromptu playing only at Kooloobong.

The Wollongong Music Camp was the brainchild of the late Nils Korner who was also a founding member of the society. It was he who had the idea for a residential music camp, and he who searched for suitable venues, ultimately finding the University of Wollongong and the Conservatorium of Music. Without his inspiration and diligence there would be no ACMS Wollongong Music Camp which has given so many members such pleasure over many years. We owe him a debt of gratitude.

Rod Tuson volunteered to produce the Newsletter.
Sunset Concerts at Kirribilli instigated at the suggestion of Sy Bluhm. $2 entry included wine and cheese. Concert on November 23 was followed by a Christmas party.
Membership Fee $15.00, Membership grew to 119.

Committee Roles & Duties

How the society operates. If you are interested in volunteering, contact the Secretary inside the Members Area.

Elected Core Roles

  • President: Chairs committee meetings, serves as the public face of the society, prepares newsletters and reports.
  • Secretary: Handles minutes of meetings, manages record keeping, and logs external correspondence.
  • Treasurer: Handles annual subscriptions, balances bank transactions, and logs expenses.

Co-opted Specialized Roles

  • Administrator: Coordinates registrations, Google Admin consoles, and keeps databases synchronized.
  • Camp Organiser: Establishes venue contracts, manages accommodation blocks, and processes brochures.
  • Group Organiser: Assembles play-groups, pairs similar levels, and evaluates balance of wind/string elements.
  • Librarian: Oversees postage of physical music parts and verifies complete sets on returns.
