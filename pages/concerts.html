Kirribilli Neighborhood Centre

Our intimate chamber recitals are held bi-monthly on Sundays at 3:00 PM in the beautiful upstairs Gallery Room of the Kirribilli Centre (16 Fitzroy St, Kirribilli).

Audience Entry: $15.00 ($10.00 concession). Payment is collected at the eastern stairway or the lift vestibule. Ticket prices include access to premium refreshments, wines, juices, and cheeses served immediately after in the gallery.

Transit & Parking Instructions:

Train: Just a brief 5-minute stroll from Milson's Point Station.

Ferry: From Milson's Point Wharf (Circular Quay takes 5 mins; Balmain takes 9 mins).

Car: Street parking is extremely tight. Allow ample time to seek parking.

Preparing For A Performance

By Lorraine Moxey (Original publication, January 1992)

Performing is the most rewarding thing a musician can do. However, a successful chamber recital demands thorough pacing and group discipline. Review these steps to prepare your mind and body for the stage.

Buffer your preparation: Allow a full three months of preparation for an unfamiliar chamber piece assuming 1 rehearsal per week.

Pick manageable limits: A simple, beautiful movement performed flawlessly is infinitely better than a complex, frantic movement played poorly.

Erase distractions: Wipe clean any prior pencil markings or fingerings before rehearsals begin, highlighting tricky transitions with a soft pencil icon.

Slow, Soft Tuning: Always tune softly, particularly string players. Once in tune, sit quietly. Tuning is not a time to perform personal cadenzas.

Establish Seating Lines: Place chairs so that peripheral vision connects all musicians. Low music stands prevent barrier blocks.

Tolerate Constructive Critique: Chamber groups are ultimate democracies. Try disputes both ways and make determinations objectively. Do not let egos damage music.

Taming Physicality: Shaking limbs, perspiration, and dry throat are perfectly natural adrenaline responses. Nerves give performance an incredible 'edge' if focused positively.

Breathe Before Starting: When sitting on stage, take 5 extra seconds. Breathe slowly to steady your pulse. Establish key templates mentally before the leader bows.

Mistake Immunity: Never grimace or frown when a part is dropped. 90% of the audience never notices; the remaining 10% are rooting for you. Put double the energy into the following bar!

Kirribilli Concert Organiser Checklist

Running our concert series is a rewarding privilege. Below is the step-by-step checklist to coordinate a bi-monthly recital program.

1
Verify Financial Membership: Ensure all participating musicians are active, paid financial members of ACMS. Encourage non-financial or external players to pay dues via the register system prior to concert date.
2
Draft Program & Notes: Aim for approximately 60–70 minutes of total playing duration. Forward electronic program outlines to coordinator Felicia Mitchell at least 2 weeks prior, and print 40 paper program booklets to place at the entrance table.
3
Sound-Checks and Venue Open: The Gallery Room is unlocked at 2:00 PM. Rehearsals and sound-checks should wind down by 2:40 PM so guests can settle in for a prompt 3:00 PM start.
4
Welcome & Acknowledgment: Start the program by welcoming the audience and reading the traditional Acknowledgement of Country. Remind listeners of the delicious post-concert refreshments, and request their help stacking the gallery chairs against the walls at 4:30 PM.
